Midterm Project Overview

A literature-review project. You may work individually or in teams.

Midterm Project Requirements

  • Select a paper from the required reading list and thoroughly read it. Explore the papers that cite this paper and find a research stream to write a literature review about. This literature review must evaluate at least one article from the reading list and three additional articles.
  • Submit a written report summarizing the literature review, including critiques and your proposed research questions. Additionally, you will need to give a 15-minute presentation (+ 5-minute Q&A) summarizing the key points of your review, your expected contributions, and your plans of action.
  • Students can do the project individually or form teams to finish this task.
  • Please register your group information on this page: Group Registration Sheet.
  • Please submit a compressed file (zip file) containing your report and slides.
  • Suggestion: You may use Google Scholar, the CUHKSZ Library, and sci-hub to search articles. You can try Zotero or EndNote for literature and reference management. For report format, you can try APA 7th edition to make your report more appealing and professional.

Deadline: Report and slides due Oct 29, 7:59 am; in-class presentations on Oct 29.

Final Project Overview

Pick up your midterm proposal and make empirical progress. You may continue individually or in the same team.

Final Project Requirements

Presentation (15 minutes + 5 minutes Q&A): Submit your PowerPoint by Dec 11, end of day. Suggested structure: Motivation, Related Work, Your Contribution, Methodology, Results, and Conclusion/Future Work.

Report (PDF) + Code/Data ZIP: Submit by Dec 12, end of day

Code Submission: Include a README explaining how to run the code, required dependencies/environments, and descriptions of key files or folders.

Evaluation: Contribution & Innovation, Methodological Soundness, Results & Analysis, Code Quality & Reproducibility, and Presentation Quality.
